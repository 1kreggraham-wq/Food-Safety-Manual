Oak Bay Beach Hotel Culinary Food Safety Guide

The Chef's Daily Interactive Reference

Section 1: Personal Responsibility (CLEAN)

This section covers the most important rules for every team member. Personal hygiene is the first line of defense against foodborne illness. These are non-negotiable standards for our luxury environment.

Rule 1: Always Wash Your Hands

When You MUST Wash:

  • **Immediately:** After using the restroom.
  • **Before:** Starting work, putting on gloves, touching ready-to-eat food.
  • **After:** Handling raw meat/fish, garbage, or touching your hair, face, or phone.
  • **Between:** Switching tasks.
  • **Before:** Tasting food (use a clean spoon every time).

The 20-Second Method:

  1. Wet hands and arms with hot running water.
  2. Apply soap and lather thoroughly (up to elbows).
  3. Scrub for **20 seconds**.
  4. Rinse completely.
  5. Dry using a single-use paper towel.

Rule 2: Report Illness

If you have vomiting, diarrhea, or a fever, DO NOT come to work.

  • You must be symptom-free for at least **48 hours** (BC Health requirement).
  • Tell your Sous Chef immediately if you feel sick during your shift.

Rule 3: Appearance & Attire

  • Wear a **clean uniform, apron, and hat/hairnet** every day.
  • No jewelry (except a plain wedding band).
  • No false nails or nail polish (they harbor bacteria).

Section 2: Temperature Control (COOK & CHILL)

Temperature is the single most effective tool we have to control bacteria. This section covers all critical temperatures, from receiving to serving. Our goal is to keep food out of the "Danger Zone" as much as possible.

The Temperature Danger Zone

Safe Cold Zone Below 4°C (40°F)
DANGER ZONE 4°C to 60°C
Safe Hot Zone Above 60°C (140°F)

Minimum Internal Cooking Temperatures

Use a calibrated probe thermometer to check the thickest part of the food.

Rapid Cooling Protocol (Max 6 Hours)

Never put large, hot containers directly into the cooler. Use shallow metal pans, ice baths, or a blast chiller.

Step 1: First 2 Hours

Cool from 60°C (140°F) down to 20°C (70°F).

This is the most critical stage.

Step 2: Next 4 Hours

Cool from 20°C (70°F) down to 4°C (40°F).

Failure Rule: If you miss these times, the food **must be thrown away** (or reheated once to 74°C and restarted).

Receiving & Storage

  • **Receiving:** Cold food must arrive at or below **4°C (40°F)**. Frozen food must be solid. Log all temps.
  • **Refrigeration:** All coolers must keep food at or below **4°C (40°F)**. Log temps twice daily.

Hot Holding

  • **Buffets & Pass:** Food must be held at or above **60°C (140°F)**.
  • **Action:** Check and log temperatures every **2 hours**.

Dishwasher Sanitization Temp

The machine must reach a final rinse temperature of at least **77°C (171°F)** to guarantee sanitization.

Section 3: Preventing Contamination (CROSS-CONTAMINATION)

Cross-contamination is when bacteria from one food (like raw chicken) transfers to another (like fresh lettuce). This section details the critical rules for separation and cleaning to prevent this.

Rule 1: Raw Food Separation

Storage: Bottoms Up!

In the cooler, store raw meat, poultry, and fish on the **lowest shelves**. This prevents juices from dripping onto ready-to-eat (RTE) food.

Rule 2: Cleaning and Sanitizing

**Cleaning** removes food and dirt. **Sanitizing** kills bacteria. You MUST do both.

3-Sink Setup

  1. WASH (Hot, soapy water)
  2. RINSE (Clear, hot water)
  3. SANITIZE (Approved chemical solution)

Use test strips to check sanitizer concentration daily.

Chemical Storage

Store ALL chemicals away from and below food and prep areas.

Section 4: Allergen Safety

For our luxury guests, allergy management is a critical part of safety and service. We treat every allergy with the highest level of care. There are no shortcuts.

Our 4-Step Allergy Protocol

  1. Check the Ticket: When a ticket is flagged for an allergy, **read it twice** and verbally confirm the allergy with the Sous Chef.
  2. Clean Setup: Clear your workstation. Use fresh, clean, and **dedicated** utensils, cutting boards, and pans.
  3. Separate Ingredients: Only use ingredients verified as safe and stored in their original, sealed containers. Prevent all cross-contact.
  4. Final Check: Use a designated, clean plate and have the Sous Chef or Exec Chef verify the finished dish before it leaves the kitchen.

Section 5: BC Regulatory Essentials

This section covers our specific legal responsibilities under British Columbia's regulations. This includes our required training, our legal logging (due diligence), and how to handle emergencies.

Required Training

FOODSAFE Level 1 Certification

As per BC law, at least **one employee with a valid FOODSAFE Level 1** certificate must be present in the kitchen during all hours of operation.

Crisis: Loss of Power

  1. Stop everything.
  2. Keep all fridge/freezer doors **closed**.
  3. Chef monitors and logs internal temps every 4 hours.
  4. Dispose of any cold food held above 4°C for over 4 hours.
